Diabetes and a Healthy Heart

A guide to managing and preventing cardiovascular disease in people with diabetes




Australian Diabetes Council 2013

What is cardiovascular disease?

Cardiovascular disease refers to a group of disorders that involve the heart and blood vessels (veins and arteries).

The most common forms of cardiovascular disease include ischaemic heart disease; affecting the vessels in the heart related to heart attack, and cerebrovascular disease; affecting the vessels in the brain related to stroke. Cardiovascular disease is the leading cause of death in Australia. People with diabetes are 3-4 times more likely to develop cardiovascular disease than those without diabetes.

General symptoms of cardiovascular disease include:

  • Chest pain – especially if repeatedly during activity or exertion
  • Shortness of breath – either for no reason or greater than expected for the activity being performed
  • Heart palpitations or irregular beats
  • Erectile dysfunction

Unfortunately, it is far more common that symptoms become noticeable during an acute event like a heart attack or stroke.

Common symptoms of heart disease or attack include

  • Chest discomfort or pain – this can feel like a tight ache, pressure, fullness or squeezing in the centre of your chest lasting more than a few minutes. This may come and go.
  • Shortness of breath – either for no reason or greater than expected for the activity being performed. This often occurs before chest discomfort or pain develops if it does at all.
  • Anxiety – feeling a sense of doom or panic for no apparent reason
  • Light-headedness – in addition to chest pressure, dizziness or fainting.
  • Sweating – sudden sweat with cold, clammy skin.
  • Nausea and vomiting

It is important to note that one may experience all, some, or none of these symptoms during a heart attack. In fact, people with diabetes, and especially those who have had it for a long time, are at increased risk of silent ischaemia where there is no pain or discomfort associated with heart attack due to reduced nerve sensation around the heart.

Common symptoms of stroke:

  • Trouble with walking – sudden dizziness, stumbling, loss of balance or coordination.
  • Trouble with speaking and understanding– confusion slurring of speech or inability to speak or think of the right words to explain what is happening (aphasia).
  • Paralysis or numbness on one side of your face or body– sudden weakness, numbness or even paralysis on one side of the body.
  • Trouble with seeing in one or both eyes – sudden onset of double, blurred or blackened vision.
  • Headache – a sudden, severe headache especially combined with any of the other symptoms.

    • As the actual symptoms of cardiovascular disease can be wide ranging or even completely absent, it is important to know what increases the risk of developing cardiovascular disease and what can be done to reduce your risk.
